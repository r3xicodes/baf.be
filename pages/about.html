Our Legacy

The Belgian Air Force (Force Aérienne Belge / Luchtmacht) is the aerial warfare branch of Belgium, responsible for safeguarding national airspace, supporting ground and naval operations, and contributing to international security missions. The modern Belgian Air Force was formally reestablished on 11 February 2025, replacing the previous Belgian Air Force designation. It is currently commanded by Lieutenant General Madeleine van der Meer, who assumed command on 7 April 2026.

Headquartered at Quartier Reine Elisabeth in Evere, Brussels, the Belgian Air Force operates multiple wings and squadrons focused on air superiority, multirole strike operations, air defense, close air support, strategic airlift, tactical airlift, helicopter operations, pilot training, and advanced surveillance capabilities. The force maintains an independent air defense posture with cooperative relationships with European air forces.

Core Values

National Sovereignty

Protecting Belgian airspace and defending national interests through air power operations and territorial security operations.

International Cooperation

Maintaining independent command structure while fostering cooperative relationships with European air forces and international partners.

Professional Excellence

Maintaining the highest standards of military readiness, training, and operational effectiveness across all command elements.

Vision and Mission

Vision

The most trusted air and space force in the world, delivering lethal combat power and reliable service to the nation, our allies, and our partners.

Mission

The Belgian Air Force protects and defends Belgium through air and space power operations, safeguards national airspace, maintains sovereign air defense, and contributes to international security and peacekeeping missions through independent capabilities.
