Air Force History

Heritage & Tradition

History

Sometime around the afternoon on 11 February 2025, Air France 2281, KLM562, and Le Pilote Québécois formally discussed an independence movement in the Low Countries that prompted the drafting of the Eylstadt Charter. The joint charter declared independence of the nations of the Low Countries from the Kingdom of Europe, called for a strong and cordial alliance with France, and proposed a confederation under one country.

During the drafting of the charter, the three agreed to be assigned to one country to govern. Air France 2281 agreed to take charge of Belgium, crowning himself Prince Versailles of Belgium. The simultaneous revival of the Royal Netherlands Air Force and the Royal Netherlands Navy marked the start of the build-up of the forces.

Since 11 February 2025, the Belgian Air Component has been taking shape as it prepares its forces for a war of independence. Following the signing of the Eylstadt Charter on 7 March 2025, the force, along with the units of the Benelux Confederation Forces, were activated on 8 March 2025.

Formation and Early Development

See also: History of the Belgian Air Component.

The origins of the modern Belgian Air Force trace back to early 2025, during a period of political upheaval in the Low Countries. On 11 February 2025, the Belgian Air Component was established under the leadership of Air France 2281 as part of a broader independence movement in the region. This effort was formalized with the signing of the Eylstadt Charter on 7 March 2025.

Activated on 8 March 2025, the Air Component began as a rapidly organized force focused on building operational capacity, recruiting personnel, and establishing air defense capabilities. During its early months, it prioritized forming a functional command structure and preparing for potential conflict tied to the independence movement.

Reorganization and Restoration

On 7 April 2026, Lieutenant General Madeleine van der Meer assumed command and initiated a comprehensive restructuring of the service. A key outcome of this reform was the restoration of the historic Belgian Air Force designation, replacing the interim “Air & Space Component” title used during its transitional phase.

This marked a shift from a provisional force into a more professional and standardized military branch, with increased emphasis on centralized command, operational readiness, and long-term development.

Modern Role

Today, the Belgian Air Force serves as the primary aerial defense branch of Belgium. It continues to expand its capabilities while maintaining a focus on national sovereignty, airspace security, and the modernization of its operational structure.
