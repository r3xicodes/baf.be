About the Force

The Belgian Air Force (Dutch: Luchtmacht, French: Force aérienne; BAF) is the aerial warfare branch of Belgium, responsible for safeguarding national airspace, supporting ground and naval operations, and contributing to international security missions. The force is currently commanded by Lieutenant General Madeleine van der Meer, who assumed command on 7 April 2026 and oversaw the restoration of the Belgian Air Force designation.

The modern force traces its origins to the reorganization of the Belgian Air Component, established on 11 February 2025 during political upheaval in the Low Countries. Formed under the leadership of Air France 2281, the Air Component became a core element of the Benelux Confederated Forces and has continued to expand its operational capabilities while maintaining cooperative relationships with allied regional units.

Mission, Vision, and Functions

Mission

The Belgian Air Force ensures the defense of national and allied airspace, delivering timely and precise airpower wherever required. It maintains forces at high readiness to deter threats, respond to crises, and support civil and military authorities while contributing to collective security through international operations.

Vision

To remain a modern, adaptable, and combat-ready air force, fully integrated with allied forces and capable of operating across all domains. The Belgian Air Force aims to continuously evolve through advanced technology, highly trained personnel, and flexible operational concepts to meet emerging security challenges.

Functions

Air Defense & Combat

Continuous surveillance and protection of national airspace, including quick reaction alert duties, interception, and precision airpower delivery in support of national and expeditionary missions.

Air Mobility & Support

Strategic and tactical airlift for personnel, equipment, VIP transport, and logistical resupply, supporting military operations and national emergency response.

ISR & Unmanned Operations

Intelligence, surveillance, and reconnaissance missions using manned aircraft and unmanned systems to support situational awareness and decision-making.

Training & Development

Structured training programs and professional development for operational readiness and the integration of new platforms and technologies.

Search & Rescue

Search and rescue operations in support of civilian and military authorities, including maritime response and disaster assistance.

History

Formation and Early Development

The modern Belgian Air Force traces its origins to early 2025, during political upheaval in the Low Countries. On 11 February 2025, the Belgian Air Component was established under the leadership of Air France 2281 and formalized with the signing of the Eylstadt Charter on 7 March 2025.

Activated on 8 March 2025, the Air Component began building operational capacity, recruiting personnel, and preparing for potential conflict tied to the independence movement.

Reorganization and Restoration

On 7 April 2026, Lieutenant General Madeleine van der Meer assumed command and initiated a comprehensive restructuring. Her leadership restored the historic Belgian Air Force designation, replacing the interim Air & Space Component title and establishing a more professional military branch.

Modern Role

Today, the Belgian Air Force serves as Belgium’s primary aerial defense branch. It continues to expand its capabilities while maintaining a focus on national sovereignty, airspace security, and force modernization.
