Bases & Installations

Belgian Air Force Military Facilities

Major Air Bases

Kleine-Brogel Air Base

Located in the Limburg province of northern Belgium, Kleine-Brogel Air Base is one of the primary operational centers of the Belgian Air Force and hosts the 2nd Tactical Wing. The base maintains Quick Reaction Alert (QRA) capabilities and close air support squadrons.

  • Location: Limburg, Belgium
  • Wing: 2nd Tactical Wing
  • Units: 3rd Fighter Squadron (QRA/Air Defense), 4th Fighter Squadron (Close Air Support)
  • Primary Aircraft: F-16 Fighting Falcon
  • Role: Air Defense & Tactical Operations

Florennes Air Base

Located in the Hainaut province of southern Belgium, Florennes Air Base is home to the 1st Tactical Wing, the primary fighter operations center of the Belgian Air Force. The base hosts advanced fighter squadrons equipped with F-16 Fighting Falcon aircraft.

  • Location: Hainaut Province, Belgium
  • Wing: 1st Tactical Wing
  • Units: 1st Fighter Squadron (Air Superiority), 2nd Fighter Squadron (Multirole Strike)
  • Primary Aircraft: F-16 Fighting Falcon
  • Role: Fighter Operations and Training

Melsbroek Air Base

Located near Brussels and serving as the headquarters location (Quartier Reine Elisabeth in Evere), Melsbroek Air Base is home to the 15th Air Transport Wing. The base serves as a command hub and operates strategic and tactical airlift operations.

  • Location: Brussels Region, Belgium
  • Wing: 15th Air Transport Wing
  • Units: 20th Transport Squadron (Strategic Airlift), 21st Transport Squadron (Tactical Airlift)
  • Primary Aircraft: Airbus A400M Atlas, C-130 Hercules
  • Role: Command, Transport & Strategic Airlift

Beauvechain Air Base

Located in the Hainaut province, Beauvechain Air Base hosts the Air Mobility Wing and Training Command. The base maintains helicopter operations, search and rescue capabilities, and advanced pilot training programs.

  • Location: Hainaut, Belgium
  • Wings: Air Mobility Wing, Training Command
  • Units: 40th/41st Helicopter Squadrons (NH90), 50th Training Squadron (Pilot Training)
  • Primary Aircraft: NH90 NFH Helicopter, SF-260 Trainer Aircraft
  • Role: Helicopter Operations & Training

Base Infrastructure

Each major Belgian Air Force base is equipped with modern facilities including:

  • Advanced flight operations centers
  • Maintenance and logistics facilities
  • Officer and enlisted personnel quarters
  • Training simulators and classrooms
  • Medical and emergency response facilities
  • Communications and command centers

International Cooperation

Belgian Air Force bases maintain independent command while hosting regular joint exercises and training operations with allied air forces across Europe. These installations serve as vital nodes in European air defense capabilities.
